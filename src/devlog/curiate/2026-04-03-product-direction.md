**Date:** 2026-04-03

## The tension

The current specs frame Curiate as a creator monetization platform — true fans, 1000 true fans, direct payments. But the name encodes something different. *Curiate* comes from *curia* (Roman assembly — deliberate collective judgment). To curate is to exercise discernment, not just preference.

Three directions are in play and haven't been resolved yet:

**Direction A — Creator platform (true fans).** Help independent musicians and visual artists find and sustain their first 1000 paying fans. Algorithmic transparency, built-in monetization, 90% to creators. The tension: discovery is still unsolved without an algorithm.

**Direction B — Small web discovery layer.** The small web and webrings are having a cultural resurgence. People are building personal sites again. The missing piece is a social layer that makes human editorial judgment visible and followable — RSS as the distribution primitive, interest-based clusters that form and dissolve rather than fixed groups. The tension: harder to monetize.

**Direction C — Curatorial identity as social object.** The person who curates *is* the product. Not a creator sharing work, not an algorithm — a human editor whose judgment others can follow and trust. Your curation history as a reputation and track record. More critic than taste-maker.

## What all three share

Human judgment over algorithmic curation. Ownership of distribution. The name already says what this is.

## Note on "taste"

The AI world is using "taste" as shorthand for editorial judgment right now. The concept is valid but the word will age poorly. Better terms: discernment, sensibility, judgment, point of view.

## Open questions

- Who are the first 10 users, and which direction serves them?
- Can groups be a unifying primitive across all three — filter-based, ephemeral, interest-driven clusters rather than fixed communities?
- Where does federation (ActivityPub) fit — infrastructure or feature?
